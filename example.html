How to Keep the Smelly Paws Away

Bath Time: Wash your dog regularly with a dog-friendly shampoo to keep them fresh and clean. If you're looking for a budget-friendly option, a small amount of Dreft works too- but use sparingly to avoid excess foam!

Sea Dip Fun: Let your dog enjoy a swim in the sea during summer. Afterward, make sure they dry off properly in the sun before coming indoors to prevent any remaining smells.

Dental Care: Freshen your dog’s breath with a dental chew stick- Pedigree chews are a great choice for this!

Dog Perfume: Use an eco-friendly dog perfume to keep them smelling lovely between baths.

Dog Bed Care: Wash your dog’s bed regularly to keep smells away and maintain hygiene. Use a pet-safe detergent and make sure to dry it thoroughly to prevent dampness that can lead to unpleasant smells.

Weekly Dog Care Routine

Brush your dog's fur at least once a week, do it more often when they are moulting. I bet you will notice less hair in your living room!

Give your furry friend a dental chewing stick every night.

Check the paws for dirt, long nails or other discomforts.

Examine the ears for wax buildup, redness, or unusual smells. Clean them with a dog friendly ear cleaner.
